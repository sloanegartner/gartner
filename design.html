SHERPA

User Interaction Design Project // 2019-2020

SHERPA is a financial app that I designed for my User Experience class in the School of Media Arts and Design. While in the class, I studied web and mobile interactions and design principles including usability, interactive design strategy, wireframing, and web and mobile app prototype development. I used Adobe XD to create the wireframe as well as the low and high fidelity parts of the project. I then used Adobe InDesign for creating the graphics and putting together the final report of the app. SHERPA is designed to make money management and tracking easier. With so many different ways to pay; Apple Pay, Venmo, PayPal, Uber, etc, it is hard for people to keep track of electronic payments and cash payments. SHERPA links all forms of electronic payments into one easy to use interface. Much like how a Sherpa is experienced in helping travelers prepare and achieve their goal of reaching high summits, SHERPA, the app, helps those who are unskilled or uncomfortable with money management reach a goal of confidence and understanding with their money. Through this project, I learned the process of user interaction and protoyping for a user friendly interface.

Music Lyrics Soul

Web and Graphic Design Project // 2020

The client wanted a site displaying his music and offering music lessons. I designed the logo as well as worked with Squarespace's features on the design and layout of the webiste. Through the process I learned about Squarespace and how to work with a client to ensure that the site meets all the needs. Since my client is a pianist, I wanted the site to have a "Rhapsody in Blue" theme to it. I had the site's color scheme be blue, purple, and gray, and the logo resemeble a retro 1960's blues typography with a note sheet behind his initials. This was a different project for me becasue I wasn't assinged it, there was no rubric to check if I did everything right. I enjoyed the creative freedom and learning about how to use Squarespace.

The AleHouse

Interactive Design Web Project // 2020

This project was for my introduction to web design class in the School of Media Arts and Design. My task was to choose a website and redesign it as I saw fit. I chose the websites that gave information on the Breweries and Wineries in the Harrisonburg and Rockingham County area. Instead of choosing one website, I decided to choose multiple sites and make "The AleHouse", a one stop shop for users to find all the information they need on the topic of drinks. Besides the wood background and drink labels, the images are my own as well as the design layout. Through this project I learned about Boostrap and the grid structure as well as Boostraps other features, such as drop down menus, Jumbotron, contact form, and buttons.
