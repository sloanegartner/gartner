UX/UI Projects

SHERPA

User Interaction Design Project // 2019-2020

User Research
Web and Mobile Interaction Design
Usability
Wireframing and Prototype Development

SHERPA is a financial app that I designed for my User Experience class in the School of Media Arts and Design. While in the class, I studied web and mobile interactions and design principles including usability, interactive design strategy, wire-framing, and web and mobile app prototype development. I used Adobe XD to create the wireframe as well as the low and high fidelity parts of the project. I then used Adobe InDesign for creating the graphics and putting together the final report of the app. SHERPA is designed to make money management and tracking easier. With so many different ways to pay; Apple Pay, Venmo, PayPal, Uber, etc, it is hard for people to keep track of electronic payments and cash payments. SHERPA links all forms of electronic payments into one easy to use interface. Much like how a Sherpa is experienced in helping travelers prepare and achieve their goal of reaching high summits, SHERPA, the app, helps those who are unskilled or uncomfortable with money management reach a goal of confidence and understanding with their money. Through this project, I learned the process of user interaction and prototyping for a user friendly interface.
